# Code of Conduct

OpenInterpretability adopts the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/).

## Summary — what it means here

- **Be kind.** Interpretability is a young field; many contributors are students or first-time researchers. Kindness is a feature, not a constraint.
- **Be honest.** Report what actually happened in your experiment. Honest negatives are welcome; spin is not.
- **Be helpful.** If you see someone struggling in Discussions, on an issue, or in a PR review — say the thing you wish someone had said to you.
- **Assume good faith.** If a PR or comment could be read two ways, pick the generous reading.

## What's not OK

Harassment, demeaning language, doxing, or any form of discrimination based on identity. Intentional bad-faith technical content (fabricated benchmark numbers, plagiarized notebooks, off-topic self-promotion).

## Enforcement

Report issues to **hi@openinterp.org** or DM a maintainer on GitHub. Reports are confidential. Maintainers will respond within 72h with one of: no action needed, private warning, temporary ban, permanent ban. Patterns matter more than one-off incidents.

## Scope

This Code applies to all spaces tied to OpenInterpretability: issues, PRs, Discussions, our Discord (when it exists), mentions on X tagging `@openinterp`, and in-person events where the project is represented.

## Adoption

Adopted 2026-04-23. Updates to this document require a PR and two-maintainer approval.
